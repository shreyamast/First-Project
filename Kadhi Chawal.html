Indian Chinese Maharastrian Street Food Dessert
Kadhi Chawal

INGREDIENTS

- Servings 4
FOR KADHI
  • 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  • 2 cups yogurt (curd)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 2 cups water
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
FOR CHAWAL
  • 2 cups Basmati rice, washed and soaked
  • 4 cups Water
  • 2-3 Green cardamom
  • 1-2 Cinnamon sticks
  • 1 tablespoon Ghee
  • Salt to taste
  1. In a bowl, mix besan, yogurt, ginger, green chili, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Add water gradually to form a smooth batter. Ensure there are no lumps. Heat oil in a large pot. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Let them splutter. Pour the besan-yogurt mixture into the pot, stirring continuously to avoid lumps. Add 2 cups of water and continue stirring. Bring the kadhi to a boil and then simmer on low heat for about 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally. The kadhi should thicken.

  2. For Pakoras (Optional) : In a separate bowl, mix besan, chopped onion, red chili powder, and salt. Add water gradually to form a thick batter. Heat oil in a pan for frying. Drop small portions of the batter into the hot oil and fry until the pakoras are golden brown. Drain excess oil on a paper towel.

  3. Assembly: If you made pakoras, add them to the kadhi just before serving. Garnish the kadhi with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with steamed basmati rice.
