Indian Chinese Maharastrian Street Food Dessert
Schzewan Fried Rice

INGREDIENTS

- Servings 4
FOR FRIED RICE
  • 2 cups cooked basmati rice (preferably refrigerated)
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, peas, corn), finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup cabbage, finely shredded
  • 1/4 cup bell peppers (red and green), finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup spring onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons Schezwan sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tomato ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili flakes (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper powder
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  1. Ensure that the cooked basmati rice is cold and not sticky. You can use leftover rice or refrigerate freshly cooked rice for a few hours. Finely chop all the vegetables and keep them ready. Heat oil in a wok or a large pan over medium-high heat. Add ginger-garlic paste and saut√© for a minute until fragrant. Add the finely chopped mixed vegetables, shredded cabbage, and bell peppers. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender but still crisp.


  2. Push the vegetables to one side of the wok. On the empty side, add Schezwan sauce and soy sauce. Mix the sauces and let them cook for 1-2 minutes. Combine the sauces with the stir-fried vegetables. Add the cold, cooked basmati rice to the wok. Gently break up any clumps using a spatula. Drizzle tomato ketchup over the rice and mix well to coat the rice with the sauces and vegetables. Continue to stir-fry for another 5-7 minutes until the rice is heated through and well-coated with the sauces.


  3. Sprinkle red chili flakes, black pepper powder, and salt to taste. Adjust the spice levels according to your preference. Mix everything thoroughly and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes. Add finely chopped spring onions and toss the fried rice for a minute. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Serve Schezwan Veg Fried Rice hot on its own or with a side of Manchurian or any favorite Indo-Chinese dish.
